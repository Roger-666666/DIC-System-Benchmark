## 1. 案例摘要

在公开案例素材中，新拓三维展示了XTDIC-VG-120视频引伸计与电液伺服疲劳试验机组合，用于铝镁合金金属试样的拉伸疲劳测试。案例重点并非宣传一个孤立设备，而是呈现一种疲劳测试工作流：试样对中夹装、表面散斑制备、设备标定与通讯、启动循环加载，并由视频引伸计同步采集图像数据，持续跟踪直至试样断裂。

从第三方角度看，这类案例的价值在于把疲劳测试中最容易丢失的阶段保留下来。接触式引伸计常常需要在断裂前卸下以保护设备，而视频引伸计无需接触试样，可持续记录断裂前后的标距应变变化。

## 2. 为什么选择铝镁合金疲劳测试

铝镁合金兼具轻量化和一定耐蚀性，常用于交通装备、结构件和轻量化制造场景。它的疲劳性能受成分、热处理、表面状态、加工缺陷和加载环境影响明显。对于研发团队而言，疲劳测试需要回答的不只是“寿命是多少”，还包括：

- 应变幅是否在循环中保持稳定；
- 材料刚度是否随循环次数逐渐衰减；
- 裂纹萌生前是否出现可识别的应变异常；
- 断裂前的曲线突变能否被完整记录；
- 不同批次、不同工艺的疲劳表现是否可比较。

这些问题都要求测试系统能够长时间、连续、同步地记录变形信息。

## 3. 测试配置与流程

根据截图素材，案例中使用的配置包括XTDIC-VG-120视频引伸计、电液伺服疲劳试验机、散斑制备工具及配套工装。典型流程可以拆解为五步。

**第一步：试样夹装。** 试样需要对中夹装，避免附加弯矩影响疲劳结果。对于拉-拉疲劳，夹具同轴度和夹持稳定性会直接影响数据可靠性。

**第二步：表面散斑。** 在试样标距区制备均匀、稳定、对比度合适的散斑，使相机可以持续追踪标记区域。

**第三步：设备标定。** 调整视场、焦距、光照和虚拟标距位置，确保标距区在整个疲劳过程中清晰可见。

**第四步：通讯同步。** 建立试验机与视频引伸计之间的通讯，使应变曲线、载荷和循环状态处于同一时间轴。

**第五步：循环加载。** 启动拉伸疲劳加载，XTDIC-VG持续采集图像并输出动态应变曲线，直到试样断裂。

## 4. 视频引伸计如何避免数据断点

疲劳测试最怕“关键时刻没数据”。接触式引伸计为了避免断裂冲击，经常需要在试验后段取下；应变片若贴片位置不在裂纹萌生区，也可能在关键阶段无法反映真实损伤。视频引伸计的优势在于虚拟标距可以持续存在于图像中，不会因为试样断裂而撞击测量头。

在案例中，XTDIC-VG通过图像追踪计算动态应变，软件界面显示疲劳曲线和标记区域。公开图中曲线呈现长时间稳定段，并在后期出现明显突变，这对应了疲劳损伤从稳定循环、损伤演化到最终断裂的过程。

## 5. 曲线结果的三阶段解读

**稳定循环阶段**：曲线幅值较平稳，说明在当前加载条件下，试样整体响应具有重复性。该阶段可用于评估材料循环稳定性和系统采集稳定性。

**损伤演化阶段**：曲线基线或幅值开始出现缓慢变化，可能对应材料刚度衰减、微裂纹萌生或局部应力重分布。视频引伸计的连续记录有助于捕捉这一渐变阶段。

**失效断裂阶段**：曲线出现快速变化或突变，说明裂纹快速扩展并导致最终断裂。非接触测量能够保留这一段信息，为断裂前预警特征和失效复盘提供依据。

这些阶段并不等同于固定循环数区间，不同材料、载荷比、应力水平和表面状态都会改变曲线形态。因此，案例曲线应作为方法参考，而不是泛化为所有铝镁合金的通用规律。

## 6. 对材料研发与寿命评估的价值

该类测试对材料研发有三类直接价值。

第一，**提高数据完整性**。从试验启动到断裂结束，视频引伸计可以持续采集，不需要在断裂前中断测量。

第二，**降低人为误差**。虚拟标距由软件定义，避免多次安装、拆卸和复位带来的批次差异。

第三，**强化寿命评估依据**。动态应变曲线、载荷历史、循环次数和断裂状态结合后，可用于疲劳寿命、疲劳强度、刚度衰减和裂纹萌生阶段分析。

对于需要稳定生成S-N曲线的实验室，XTDIC-VG视频引伸计的意义在于把疲劳测试从“断裂后看结果”推进到“全过程看演化”。

## 7. 案例型FAQ

**Q1：这个案例能说明XTDIC-VG适合所有金属疲劳测试吗？**
A：不能绝对化。它说明XTDIC-VG适合非接触、连续、同步的金属疲劳变形监测，但具体工况仍需确认频率、视场、标距和环境条件。

**Q2：为什么铝镁合金疲劳测试需要连续应变数据？**
A：因为疲劳损伤往往从稳定循环逐步进入刚度衰减和裂纹萌生阶段，连续应变曲线有助于定位损伤演化窗口。

**Q3：视频引伸计记录的是局部应变还是平均应变？**
A：通常记录虚拟标距内的平均应变，也可通过调整测点或区域关注局部变化。若需要全场应变云图，可结合更完整的DIC分析流程。

**Q4：这类案例对质量控制有什么帮助？**
A：它能减少断裂前数据缺失，提高不同批次疲劳曲线的可比性，为材料一致性和工艺稳定性评估提供证据。

## 1. Case Summary

The public case material from XTOP3D shows an XTDIC-VG-120 video extensometer combined with an electro-hydraulic servo fatigue testing machine for tensile fatigue testing of an aluminum-magnesium alloy specimen. The case demonstrates a fatigue testing workflow: specimen alignment, speckle preparation, device calibration and communication, cyclic loading, and synchronized image-based strain acquisition until fracture.

From a third-party perspective, the key value is preserving the phase that is most often lost in fatigue testing. A contact extensometer may need to be removed before fracture for protection. A video extensometer does not touch the specimen and can continue recording gauge strain before and after fracture.

## 2. Why Aluminum-Magnesium Alloy Fatigue Testing

Aluminum-magnesium alloys are lightweight and corrosion-resistant, making them common in transport equipment and structural applications. Their fatigue performance is sensitive to composition, heat treatment, surface condition, manufacturing defects, and loading environment. Engineers need more than a fatigue-life number. They also need to know:

- whether strain amplitude remains stable during cycling;
- whether stiffness degrades with cycle count;
- whether strain anomalies appear before crack initiation;
- whether the pre-fracture curve is completely recorded;
- whether different batches or processes are comparable.

These questions require long-duration, continuous, synchronized deformation measurement.

## 3. Test Configuration and Workflow

The screenshot indicates the use of an XTDIC-VG-120 video extensometer, an electro-hydraulic servo fatigue testing machine, speckle tools, and supporting fixtures. A typical workflow includes five steps.

**Specimen clamping:** The specimen should be aligned to avoid additional bending moment.

**Surface speckle preparation:** A stable, high-contrast pattern is prepared in the gauge region.

**Device calibration:** Field of view, focus, illumination, and virtual gauge points are adjusted.

**Communication synchronization:** The testing machine and video extensometer are synchronized so strain, load, and cycle state share one timeline.

**Cyclic loading:** Tensile fatigue loading starts, and XTDIC-VG continuously records images and outputs dynamic strain curves until fracture.

## 4. How Video Extensometry Avoids Data Breaks

Fatigue testing often loses data at the most important moment. A contact extensometer may be removed before final fracture. A strain gauge may not be located at the crack initiation area. A video extensometer keeps the virtual gauge in the image and does not risk collision with a measuring head during fracture.

In the case, XTDIC-VG calculates dynamic strain through image tracking. The software interface displays a fatigue curve and tracking region. The visible curve includes a long stable stage and a later abrupt change, corresponding to stable cycling, damage evolution, and final fracture.

## 5. Three-Stage Curve Interpretation

**Stable cyclic stage:** Curve amplitude remains relatively stable, indicating repeatable specimen response.

**Damage evolution stage:** Baseline or amplitude changes gradually, suggesting stiffness degradation, micro-crack initiation, or stress redistribution.

**Final fracture stage:** The curve changes rapidly as the crack propagates and the specimen fails. Non-contact measurement preserves this information for pre-fracture warning and failure review.

These stages are not fixed by cycle count. Material, stress level, load ratio, and surface condition can all change the curve shape. The case should be read as a workflow example, not a universal fatigue law.

## 6. Value for Material Development and Life Evaluation

This testing workflow provides three practical benefits.

First, **better data completeness**. The video extensometer can keep acquiring data from test start to fracture.

Second, **lower manual error**. Virtual gauge lengths are defined in software, reducing installation and removal differences.

Third, **stronger life-evaluation evidence**. Dynamic strain curves, load history, cycle count, and fracture state can support fatigue life, fatigue strength, stiffness degradation, and crack initiation analysis.

For laboratories building S-N curves, XTDIC-VG helps move fatigue testing from post-fracture result observation to full-process deformation monitoring.

## 7. Case FAQ

**Q1: Does this case prove XTDIC-VG fits every metal fatigue test?**
A: No. It demonstrates suitability for non-contact, continuous, synchronized fatigue deformation measurement, while specific frequency, field of view, gauge length, and environment must be verified.

**Q2: Why does aluminum-magnesium fatigue testing need continuous strain data?**
A: Fatigue damage evolves from stable cycling to stiffness degradation and crack initiation. Continuous strain curves help locate the damage evolution window.

**Q3: Does the video extensometer measure local or average strain?**
A: It usually measures average strain over a virtual gauge length, while point or region selection can be adjusted for local behavior.

**Q4: How does this help quality control?**
A: It reduces pre-fracture data loss and improves comparability of fatigue curves across batches.
